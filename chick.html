How to Fold a Traditional Origami Basketchick

The traditional paper origami basket chick is a charming and creative design that combines both a container and a figure in one model. This origami represents care, creativity, and new beginnings, as the small chick sits safely inside its folded paper basket. It is a popular design for spring themes and decorative displays, and it helps improve precision, patience, and folding accuracy while creating a delightful handmade piece.

Traditional Paper Basketchick Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Start with the colored side of the paper facing up on a flat surface.
  2. Fold the paper in half diagonally to form a triangle.
  3. Unfold and fold diagonally in the opposite direction.
  4. Turn the paper over and fold it in half horizontally.
  5. Unfold and fold it in half vertically. Crease well.
  6. Collapse the paper into a square base using the creases.
  7. Place the square so the open corner faces downward.
  8. Fold the right flap toward the center crease.
  9. Fold the left flap toward the center crease.
  10. Turn the model over and repeat both folds.
  11. Fold the top triangular flap downward.
  12. Unfold the last three folds to prepare for shaping.
  13. Lift the top layer and open it upward.
  14. Flatten the paper into a neat petal fold.
  15. Turn the model over and repeat the petal fold.
  16. You should now have a tall diamond-shaped base.
  17. Fold the bottom tip upward slightly to form the basket base.
  18. Crease firmly to hold the shape.
  19. Open the bottom gently to create a shallow basket.
  20. Fold the front tip upward to form the chick’s body.
  21. Fold a small triangle outward to create the beak.
  22. Turn the model slightly to adjust the angle.
  23. Fold two small flaps outward for the wings.
  24. Round the edges gently with your fingers.
  25. Open the basket section carefully.
  26. Adjust the sides so the chick sits securely.
  27. Shape the head by slightly reversing the top fold.
  28. Refine the basket rim evenly.
  29. Ensure the chick stands balanced in the basket.
  30. Your origami basket chick is now complete.
