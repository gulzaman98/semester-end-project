How to Fold a Traditional Origami Lotus

Lotus is a serene and elegant origami flower inspired by the purity, symmetry, and graceful form of real lotus blossoms. This refined design features smooth, precise folds that create beautifully layered petals, forming a balanced and visually captivating flower shape. Lotus origami is perfect for decoration, handmade gifts, craft projects, and creative displays. It also enhances focus, patience, creativity, and fine motor skills, offering a calm, peaceful, and deeply satisfying paper-folding experience.

Traditional Paper Lotus Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Start with the colored side of the paper facing up. Place the paper flat on the table.
  2. Fold the paper in half diagonally to form a triangle, then unfold.
  3. Fold diagonally in the opposite direction and unfold to make clear creases.
  4. Turn the paper over and fold it in half horizontally. Unfold.
  5. Fold it in half vertically and unfold to create a grid of creases.
  6. Using the creases, collapse the paper into a square base.
  7. Position the square so the open end faces downward.
  8. Fold the bottom corner upward to meet the top point.
  9. Fold the left and right corners inward toward the center line.
  10. Turn the model over and repeat the same folds on the other side.
  11. Fold the top layers downward to shape the lotus bud.
  12. Gently open the top layers outward to form the inner petals.
  13. Rotate the model 90 degrees clockwise.
  14. Fold the outer corners outward slightly to shape the broad lotus petals.
  15. Turn the model over and repeat the same folds.
  16. Carefully open and spread the layered folds to create a blooming lotus shape.
  17. Curve the edges gently to give the petals a soft and natural look.
  18. Adjust the folds to form a balanced and symmetrical flower shape.
  19. Refine the petal layers for a neat and elegant finish.
  20. Smooth out all creases carefully.
  21. Make small adjustments to improve symmetry.
  22. Shape the petals gently using your fingers.
  23. Ensure all layers are neatly aligned.
  24. Give the model a final gentle press.
  25. Your Lotus origami flower is now complete.
