How to Fold a Traditional Origami Chicken

Origami Chicken is a fun and lively paper model that captures the friendly and charming look of a real chicken. With simple yet precise folds, this design creates a plump, rounded chicken that is both visually appealing and easy to fold. Inspired by the cheerful nature of farm animals, the Origami Chicken features clean lines and balanced proportions, making it ideal for beginners and young crafters. Chicken origami is perfect for craft projects, school activities, and decorative displays, while also helping to improve focus, creativity, and fine motor skills. It provides an enjoyable and hands-on paper-folding experience that combines imagination, patience, and artistic expression in a playful way.

Traditional Paper Chicken Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Start with a square sheet of paper, colored side up, and place it flat on the table.
  2. Fold the paper diagonally in half to form a triangle, then unfold to create a crease.
  3. Fold diagonally in the opposite direction and unfold to form a second crease.
  4. Fold the paper in half horizontally and vertically, then unfold to create a grid of creases.
  5. Collapse the paper into a preliminary base (square base) using the creases.
  6. Fold the right and left edges toward the center to form a kite shape.
  7. Turn the model over and repeat the folds on the other side.
  8. Fold the top point downward slightly to shape the chicken's body.
  9. Lift the top layer and squash fold to form the chicken's head.
  10. Make a small inside reverse fold at the tip of the head to create the beak.
  11. Fold the sides outward slightly to shape the wings of the chicken.
  12. Fold a small flap at the bottom to form the tail feathers.
  13. Adjust the wings, head, and body to give the chicken a balanced, rounded appearance.
  14. Refine any creases to make the model neat and stable.
  15. Ensure all layers are aligned properly for a clean look.
  16. Your Origami Chicken is now complete and ready for display.
