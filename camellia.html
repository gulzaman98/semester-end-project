How to Fold a Traditional Origami Camellia

Camellia is a graceful and elegant origami flower design inspired by the natural beauty and soft curves of real camellia blossoms. This model features smooth, balanced folds that form delicate layered petals, creating a realistic and visually pleasing flower shape. Camellia origami is perfect for decoration, handmade gifts, craft projects, and creative displays. It also helps improve focus, patience, creativity, and fine motor skills, while providing a calm and enjoyable paper-folding experience.

Traditional Paper Camellia Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Start with the colored side of the paper facing up. Place the paper flat on the table.
  2. Fold the paper in half diagonally to form a triangle, then unfold.
  3. Fold diagonally in the opposite direction and unfold to make clear creases.
  4. Turn the paper over and fold it in half horizontally. Unfold.
  5. Fold it in half vertically and unfold to create a grid of creases.
  6. Using the creases, collapse the paper into a square base.
  7. Position the square so the open end faces downward.
  8. Fold the right and left corners toward the center line to form a kite shape.
  9. Turn the model over and repeat the same folds on the other side.
  10. Fold the top point downward slightly to shape the flower bud.
  11. Unfold the last fold and gently open the top layer.
  12. Flatten the sides inward to create soft petal layers.
  13. Rotate the model 90 degrees clockwise.
  14. Fold the outer corners inward to shape the camellia petals.
  15. Turn the model over and repeat the same folds.
  16. Gently open the layered folds to create a natural flower form.
  17. Curve the edges slightly to give the petals a soft look.
  18. Adjust the folds to form a balanced, round shape.
  19. Refine the petal layers for a neat and elegant finish.
  20. Smooth out all creases carefully.
  21. Make small adjustments to improve symmetry.
  22. Shape the petals gently using your fingers.
  23. Ensure all layers are neatly aligned.
  24. Give the model a final gentle press.
  25. Your Camellia origami flower is now complete.
