How to Fold a Traditional Origami Butterfly

The traditional paper origami butterfly is a beautiful and symbolic design that represents transformation, freedom and creativity. This elegant model is created using balanced and symmetrical folds, resulting in delicate wings that give the butterfly a lifelike appearance. Origami butterflies are popular for decoration, greeting cards, and art projects, and they help improve concentration, precision, and fine motor skills while offering a relaxing and rewarding folding experience.

Traditional Paper Butterfly Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Start with the colored side of the paper facing down.
  2. Fold the paper in half diagonally to form a triangle.
  3. Unfold and repeat the diagonal fold in the opposite direction.
  4. Flip the paper to the other side.
  5. Fold the paper in half horizontally.
  6. Unfold and fold vertically.
  7. Collapse the paper into a triangle base.
  8. Position the triangle with the open end facing down.
  9. Fold the right corner up to the top point.
  10. Fold the left corner up to the top point.
  11. Turn the model over carefully.
  12. Fold the top layer down slightly past the bottom edge.
  13. Crease firmly and hold the fold in place.
  14. Fold the model in half vertically.
  15. Adjust the layers so both sides are even.
  16. Fold the top wing diagonally downward.
  17. Repeat the same fold on the other wing.
  18. Turn the model over again.
  19. Gently open the wings outward.
  20. Adjust the wing angles for symmetry.
  21. Press the center firmly to define the body.
  22. Slightly curve the wings upward for shape.
  23. Sharpen all creases carefully.
  24. Check alignment of both wings.
  25. Refine the folds near the center.
  26. Flatten any uneven edges.
  27. Balance the butterfly on a flat surface.
  28. Make small adjustments to wing tips.
  29. Ensure the body fold is tight.
  30. Your origami butterfly is now complete.
