How to Fold a Traditional Origami Lily

The traditional paper origami lily is an elegant and graceful flower design that symbolizes purity, renewal, and beauty. This model is inspired by the natural shape of a blooming lily and is widely appreciated for its balanced folds and refined structure. Origami lilies are commonly used for decoration, gifts, and creative displays, and they help improve precision, patience, and attention to detail while offering a calming and rewarding folding experience.

Traditional Paper Lily Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Start with the colored side of the paper facing up. Place the paper flat on the table.
  2. Fold the paper diagonally in half to form a triangle.
  3. Unfold and fold diagonally in the opposite direction. Crease well.
  4. Turn the paper over and fold it in half horizontally.
  5. Unfold and fold it in half vertically. Crease firmly.
  6. Collapse the paper into a square base using the existing creases.
  7. Position the square so the open end faces downward.
  8. Fold the right flap toward the center crease.
  9. Fold the left flap toward the center crease.
  10. Turn the model over and repeat the same folds on the other side.
  11. Fold the top triangular flap downward and crease well.
  12. Unfold the last three folds completely.
  13. Lift the top layer and open it upward.
  14. Perform a petal fold by flattening the sides inward.
  15. Turn the model over and repeat the petal fold on the other side.
  16. You should now have a tall diamond-shaped base.
  17. Fold the right edge to the center line.
  18. Fold the left edge to the center line.
  19. Turn the model over and repeat both folds.
  20. Carefully open the top layers slightly.
  21. Gently pull one petal upward to form the flower shape.
  22. Repeat this step for the remaining three petals.
  23. Use your fingers to curve each petal outward.
  24. Adjust the folds to make the petals symmetrical.
  25. Turn the model upside down.
  26. Twist the bottom section slightly to tighten the base.
  27. Fold the bottom tip inward to lock the flower.
  28. Refine the petal edges for a smooth finish.
  29. Ensure the flower can stand properly.
  30. Your origami lily flower is now complete.
