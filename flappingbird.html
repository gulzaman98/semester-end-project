How to Fold a Traditional Origami Flapping Bird

Flapping Bird is a classic and interactive origami model that beautifully captures the motion of a bird in flight. Crafted with precise folds and well-balanced wings, this design creates a realistic flapping movement when the tail is gently pulled. Inspired by the elegance and freedom of flying birds, the model features clean lines and symmetrical structure, forming a graceful and lively paper creation. Flapping Bird origami is ideal for craft activities, school projects, and decorative displays, while also enhancing focus, creativity, and fine motor skills. It provides a fun, hands-on paper-folding experience that combines movement, imagination, and artistic expression.

Traditional Paper Flapping Bird Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Start with a square sheet of paper placed flat on the table with the colored side facing down.
  2. Fold the paper diagonally in half to form a triangle, then crease firmly.
  3. Fold the triangle in half again to make a smaller triangle.
  4. Unfold the last fold to return to the larger triangle shape.
  5. Lift the top layer from the bottom corner and squash fold it to form a square shape.
  6. Turn the model over and repeat the squash fold on the other side to create a square base.
  7. Position the square so the open end is facing downward.
  8. Fold the right and left edges toward the center line to form a kite shape.
  9. Turn the model over and repeat the same folds on the other side.
  10. Fold the top triangular flap downward and crease well.
  11. Unfold the last three folds to prepare for the next step.
  12. Lift the bottom point upward, opening the sides, and squash fold it along the existing creases to form a long diamond shape (bird base).
  13. Turn the model over and repeat the same fold on the other side.
  14. Fold one narrow flap upward to form the bird’s neck.
  15. Fold the other narrow flap upward to create the tail.
  16. Create the head by making a small inside reverse fold at the tip of the neck.
  17. Fold the wings downward on both sides to shape the bird.
  18. Adjust the wings so they are even and symmetrical.
  19. Gently hold the bird by the body and pull the tail outward to test the flapping movement.
  20. Make small adjustments to improve balance and smoothness.
  21. Your Flapping Bird origami is now complete.
