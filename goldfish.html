How to Fold a Traditional Origami Golden Fish

Golden Fish origami is a bright and visually appealing paper model inspired by the graceful movement and vibrant beauty of golden fish commonly found in ponds and aquariums. With its smooth body shape, flowing fins, and gently curved tail, this design captures the elegant appearance of a golden fish through clean and precise paper folds. The model features soft curves and balanced proportions that give it a decorative and realistic look. Golden Fish origami is suitable for beginners as well as intermediate folders who enjoy colorful and nature-inspired designs. It is ideal for craft projects, classroom activities, and decorative displays. Folding this model helps improve focus, patience, and fine motor skills. The symmetrical structure enhances accuracy and attention to detail. Its flowing fins and curved tail add a lively and graceful touch to the final model. Golden Fish origami offers a relaxing and enjoyable paper-folding experience that encourages creativity and artistic expression.

Traditional Paper Golden Fish Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Start with a square sheet of paper placed flat on the table, colored side facing down.
  2. Fold the paper diagonally in half to form a triangle and crease firmly.
  3. Unfold the paper back into a square.
  4. Fold diagonally in the opposite direction and crease well.
  5. Unfold again to return to the square shape.
  6. Fold the paper in half horizontally and crease.
  7. Unfold, then fold it vertically in half, crease firmly, and unfold.
  8. Using the existing creases, collapse the paper into a square base (preliminary base).
  9. Place the square base with the open end facing downward.
  10. Fold the right and left edges of the top layer toward the center line to form a kite shape.
  11. Turn the model over and repeat the same folds on the other side.
  12. Fold the top triangular flap downward and crease sharply.
  13. Unfold the last three folds to prepare for shaping the fish body.
  14. Lift the bottom point upward while opening the sides and flatten carefully to form a long diamond shape.
  15. Turn the model over and repeat the same fold on the other side.
  16. Fold one side flap upward to begin shaping the upper fin.
  17. Fold the opposite side flap slightly downward to form the lower fin.
  18. Fold the top point forward slightly to create the fish’s head.
  19. Make a small inside reverse fold at the tip to define the mouth.
  20. Fold the back section outward to begin forming the tail.
  21. Open and flatten the tail to give it a wide, fan-like shape.
  22. Curve the tail gently to create a flowing appearance.
  23. Adjust the upper and lower fins by folding them outward.
  24. Gently curve the fins using your fingers for a natural look.
  25. Refine the body by pressing along the center crease.
  26. Ensure both sides of the fish are symmetrical.
  27. Smooth out all creases for a neat and clean finish.
  28. Make small adjustments to enhance the fish’s elegant shape.
  29. Shape the tail slightly upward to give a swimming effect.
  30. Check balance and refine any uneven folds.
  31. Your Golden Fish origami is now complete and ready for display.
