How to Fold a Traditional Origami Angel FIsh

Angel Fish is a graceful and eye-catching origami model inspired by the elegant movement of tropical fish in the ocean. With its wide, flowing fins and balanced body shape, this design creates a beautiful underwater appearance using simple paper folds. The model features clean lines and smooth curves that give it a realistic and decorative look. Angel Fish origami is suitable for beginners as well as intermediate folders who enjoy creative marine designs. It is perfect for craft projects, classroom activities, and themed decorations. Folding this model helps improve concentration, patience, and fine motor skills. The symmetrical structure of the fish enhances precision and attention to detail. Its flowing fins add a dynamic and lively touch to the final result. Angel Fish origami offers a relaxing and enjoyable paper-folding experience. It combines imagination, creativity, and artistic expression in a simple yet impressive design.

Traditional Paper Angel Fish Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Start with a square sheet of paper placed flat on the table, colored side facing down.
  2. Fold the paper diagonally in half to form a triangle, then crease firmly.
  3. Unfold the triangle back into a square.
  4. Fold diagonally in the opposite direction and crease well.
  5. Unfold again to return to the square shape.
  6. Fold the paper in half horizontally and crease.
  7. Unfold and then fold it vertically in half, crease firmly, and unfold.
  8. Using the existing creases, collapse the paper into a square base (preliminary base).
  9. Place the square base with the open end facing downward.
  10. Fold the right and left edges of the top layer toward the center line to form a kite shape.
  11. Turn the model over and repeat the same folds on the other side.
  12. Fold the top triangular flap downward and crease sharply.
  13. Unfold the last three folds to prepare for a petal fold.
  14. Lift the bottom point upward while opening the sides and flatten carefully to form a long diamond shape.
  15. Turn the model over and repeat the same petal fold on the other side.
  16. Fold one narrow flap upward to begin shaping the fish’s upper fin.
  17. Fold the opposite narrow flap slightly downward to create the lower fin.
  18. Gently open one side layer and squash fold it outward to widen the body.
  19. Repeat on the other side to balance the shape.
  20. Fold the top point backward slightly to round the fish’s head.
  21. Create a small inside reverse fold at the tip to define the mouth.
  22. Fold the back section outward to shape the tail fin.
  23. Open the tail slightly and flatten it to give it a fan-like appearance.
  24. Adjust the upper and lower fins by folding them outward for a flowing effect.
  25. Gently curve the fins using your fingers to create a natural look.
  26. Refine the body by pressing along the center crease for structure.
  27. Ensure both sides are symmetrical and aligned properly.
  28. Smooth out all creases to make the model neat.
  29. Make small adjustments to enhance the overall fish shape.
  30. Shape the tail slightly upward for a lively swimming pose.
  31. Check balance and refine any uneven folds.
  32. Your Angel Fish origami is now complete and ready for display.
